## Passage 1: Our School Website Project

**Theme: Project Management & Teamwork**

Our class decided to build a school website. We used the **Agile** method to manage our work. First, we set a clear **goal**: to help students find school news easily. We divided the class into a small **team**.

Tom is the **leader**. He assigned a **task** to everyone. Lily works on the **frontend**, so she designs the **interface** and the **layout**. I work on the **backend** to manage the **database**. We have a short **stand-up** meeting every morning to check our **progress**. If someone has a **blocker**, the whole team helps. We want to finish the first **version** before the **deadline**. It is a great **collaboration**!

## Passage 2: Tips for Online Security

**Theme: Cybersecurity & Internet**

Today, many teenagers have their own social media **accounts**. However, we must care about **security**. First, never share your **password** or **credentials** with others. If a stranger sends you a **link**, do not click it easily. It might be a **phishing** trap or contain a **virus**.

Second, check your **privacy** settings. You can choose who can see your **profile** and **posts**. If you see some **spam** or feel someone is a **troll**, you can **block** or **report** them. Always keep your **software** updated to the latest **version** to fix any **vulnerabilities**. Staying safe online is very important.

**Questions:**

1. What should you do if a stranger sends you a link?
    
2. Why is it important to update software?
    
3. What can you do if you meet a "troll" online?
    
4. What does "privacy settings" help you control?
    
5. Is it okay to share your credentials with your best friend?

## Passage 3: My First Coding Experience

**Theme: Programming & Development**

Last summer, I started to learn **JavaScript**. At first, I thought it was hard to understand the **syntax**. I learned how to define a **variable** and write a simple **function**.

One day, my code had a **bug**. The **output** was not what I expected. I spent two hours trying to **debug** it. Finally, I found the **error**: I forgot a **bracket**. I used a **comment** to explain that part of the code so I wouldn't forget it later. Now, I can create a simple **application** that can **calculate** numbers. Coding is like solving a puzzle; it needs a lot of **logic**.

## Passage 4: Life on Social Media

**Theme: Social Media & Interaction**

Social media **platforms** like Facebook or Instagram change our lives. We can **upload** photos and **share** our stories with **followers**. When we post something, we wait for **likes** and **comments**. This is called **engagement**.

Sometimes, we follow an **influencer** to see the latest **trends**. However, we should not spend too much time **scrolling** through the **timeline**. Sometimes the **content** on the **feed** is not real. Some people only show their best moments. We should focus on our real life and use **messaging** apps to talk to real friends.

## Passage 5: The Magic "Cloud"

**Theme: Cloud Computing & Data**

In the past, people saved photos on a **disk**. Now, we use **cloud storage**. When you **upload** a file, it goes to a remote **server**. You can **access** your data from any **device**, like a phone or a laptop, if you have an internet **connection**.

The **cloud** is very useful for **backup**. If your computer is broken, your files are still safe in the **database**. Many companies use **SaaS** (Software as a Service) to work online. It makes our work more **flexible**. However, we need a strong **protocol** to protect our data from a **breach**.

**Questions:**

1. Where did people save photos in the past?
    
2. What do you need to access cloud data from different devices?
    
3. Why is the cloud good for "backup"?
    
4. What does SaaS stand for?
    
5. What is needed to protect data from a "breach"?
